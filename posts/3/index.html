CCNA (200-301) Experience

August 14, 2021 2-minute read

I recently passed the Cisco Certified Networking Associate (CCNA) exam. This is a great certification if you want to work in networking or IT in general. Here is some general information about the exam:

  • Question Count: Over 100 questions (102-104)
  • Question Types: Multiple choice and drag-and-drop
  • Time: 120 minutes
  • Cost: $300 USD
  • Score Required to Pass: 825 (out of 1000)

Materials Used

I used:

  • the official CCNA Course guide(Volume 1 & 2),
  • Neil Anderson’s Udemy Couse,
  • David Bombal’s Course on Udemy and his packet tracer Labs,
  • a Bit of CBTNuggets and
  • a lot of motivation from NetworkChuck and David Bombal, not forgetting the Quizzes from Keith Barker.

My Experience with the Exam

The exam was very difficult. Some questions were good. Some were really vague. For example, I got many questions involving reading routing tables. I thought these were good, as it tested your knowledge of IP addressing, subnetting, routing, etc. Questions like these really tested if you knew what you were doing, and they were satisfying problems to solve. On the other hand, I was also asked some very obscure questions: they just tested if you memorized very specific things. Overall, It was a great exam and felt they tested me on a broad range of topics.

Conclusion

I had a great experience studying for the CCNA. I’ve learned so much while studying for it, and it is extremely valuable. I am hoping for a similar experience as i prepare for the CCNP ENCOR 350-401 exams.
